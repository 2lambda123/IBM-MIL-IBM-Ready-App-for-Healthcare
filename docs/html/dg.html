Design Guidelines

Physio was created following the IBM Design Language V1 guidelines. You can see the PDF here.

Dashboard

The dashboard displays the most relevant information at a glance. The healthcare metric tabs on the right allow the user to quickly visualize their progress. The "ouch button" on the top right allows the patient to quickly file a pain management report the moment they need it. At the bottom of the dashboard the patients are able to see information regarding their next exercise to complete, and the "Get Started" button allows the patients to quickly start the exercise routine.

The background image used in the dashboard should reflect the next exercise the patient must complete. This image is used again as a thumbnail in the Exercise Library where the detailed exercise information is displayed for each routine.

Exercise Library

In the Exercise Library it is important to differentiate between the exercises that have been assigned to the patients by the Physical Therapist and those that are just part of the exercise library. When displaying the exercises in each Routine, a thumbnail image of the exercise should be displayed along with detailed information of the exercise including exercise name, and duration of exercise. Once a specific exercise is selected, a video tutorial of how to perform the exercise can be displayed along with the detailed information of the exercise, including name and duration of exercise, and any tools the patients may need.

Progress Metrics

Progress Metrics should allow the patients to view their progress across the different healthcare metrics at a glance. Also, by clicking on a specific metric it should provide the patients with a more detailed view of their progress. It is important to display not only daily patient progress, but also their progress in the last Week, Month, and Year, since this has shown to improve patient adherence to the at-home physical therapy programs. Similar metrics (for example Calories and Weight), should be displayed by using the same colors if possible to increase visual consistency throughout the app and prevent it from becoming visually overwhelming.

Pain Management

Both male and female options should be available. Visual feedback is required when the user makes a selection of an area in the body diagram. The pointers used to identify specific pain points should be limited to 5 at the most. Including a Description Box where the patients can explain further the reason of their pain is relevant. Other methods of rating the pain levels can be used but a numerical scale has been proven to be the most successful to accurately identify how the patient feels.

Forms

Questions should be reworded as Yes/No questions for easier and faster completion.
